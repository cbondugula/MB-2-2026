## **THE JOINT COMMISSION (TJC) BUSINESS MODEL ANALYSIS**

### **TJC Revenue Structure and Incentives**

**TJC Annual Revenue**: ~$400M-$500M  
**Primary Revenue Sources**:
- **Accreditation Fees**: $45K-$125K per hospital every 3 years
- **Survey Costs**: $15K-$35K per on-site visit
- **Consultation Services**: $2K-$8K per day
- **Training Programs**: $500-$2,500 per participant
- **Publications**: $50-$500 per resource

**TJC Market Position**:
- **Quasi-Monopoly**: Required for Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement
- **6,200+ Accredited Organizations**: Hospitals, surgery centers, nursing homes
- **Regulatory Power**: Sets standards that become law through CMS adoption
- **Market Control**: Essential gatekeeper for healthcare facility operations

### **TJC Strategic Challenges**

**Operational Inefficiencies**:
- **Manual Processes**: Survey preparation takes facilities 6-12 months
- **Resource Intensive**: On-site surveys require 3-7 surveyors for 3-5 days
- **High Costs**: $2M-$5M annual operational cost per surveyor team
- **Inconsistency**: Surveyor interpretation varies, creating fairness concerns

**Market Pressures**:
- **CMS Competition**: Centers for Medicare & Medicaid direct oversight
- **State Alternatives**: Some states developing independent accreditation
- **Technology Disruption**: Automated compliance threatens traditional model
- **Cost Pressure**: Hospitals demand more efficient, cost-effective accreditation
